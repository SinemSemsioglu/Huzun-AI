The HüzünProject is an interdisciplinary research project that has the aim of teaching the concept of Hüzün to a non-human entity, like an artificial agent.

ABOUT

Hüzün, a Turkish word for an emotion known for its untranslatability, is the starting point for this project. Instead of assuming what a dictionary states, this project questions the limits of language, translation and understanding in an ongoing expedition trying to find the meaning of a single word.

Taking Turkish literature as its starting point, we created an archive that aims to collect video interviews from translators, authors, linguists, psychologists among many other individuals that reflect upon Hüzün. These interviews form the base for an Artificial Intelligence architecture that tries to learn the meaning of this ungraspable word and represent it. This expedition aims to exceed the limits of the humane and asks if a machine can “share the same emotions” with humans combining many disciplines, from literature to computer science, from anthropology to design in the broadest sense of the word.

HÜZÜN.archive

The HüzünProject consists of the creation of an ongoing video archive where a wide-scale of different specialists are interviewed and shed light on their understanding of the word Hüzün.

A.I.HÜZÜN

The computational science of emotional analysis very often investigates emotions under universal categories. Most of the time, the science takes Paul Ekman’s model of six basic emotions as its base; anger, disgust, fear, happiness, sadness and surprise. Although, the universality of the emotional flux offers science and design a common ground, it also loses locality and the emotional nuances of diverse cultures and the differences in cultural histories. A.I.Hüzün takes a mechanic look at this local emotion and speculates what if Hüzün is analysed by the means of science and technology.
